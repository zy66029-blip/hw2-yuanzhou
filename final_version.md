# AI in Research, Communication, and Decision-Making

I am a master's student at Johns Hopkins Carey Business School, where I study Information Systems and AI. I am especially interested in how technology can support research, communication, and practical decision-making in academic and professional settings.

One area I care about is the use of generative AI in research and business communication. In both school and workplace environments, people often need to read large amounts of information, identify key points, and turn rough ideas into organized writing or presentations. I think generative AI is most useful as a starting tool that can improve efficiency, while human judgment is still necessary for accuracy and clarity.

## Possible Uses of Generative AI
- Summarizing articles, reports, and research materials into clear key takeaways
- Assisting with first drafts of emails, presentations, and professional writing
- Helping organize ideas and compare options during early-stage analysis
